Introduction

Black Purple is a jewelry brand based in Seoul, Korea, catering to a fashion audience aged 18–27, offering a selection of bold, stylish accessories. The brand embraces a modern, edgy, rebellious aesthetic, attracting customers who want jewelry that complements their personal style.

My Role

I was responsible for analyzing the existing website’s pain points, simplifying navigation, and improving the overall user experience. My focus was on creating a more conversion-driven design that enhances product discovery and streamlines the checkout process.

Problem

The current website suffers from cluttered navigation, making it difficult for users to browse products efficiently and complete their purchases smoothly. The excessive number of menu categories, lack of clear organization, and overwhelming layout create friction in the shopping experience, leading to potential frustration, drop-offs, and abandoned carts.

Goal

The goal is to redesign the current website user interface and improve the overall user experience, making it easier for customers to browse, shop, and complete purchases effortlessly. This redesign’s focuses is on retaining customers and increasing conversion rates by reducing friction in the shopping process.

Research

I began my research with a UI audit to identify the existing problems on the current Black Purple website. This involved evaluating the navigation structure, visual hierarchy, usability, and overall user flow to find areas that would potentially cause confusion for users.

I then researched for competitors similar BlackPurple focusing on the UI layouts, website traffic and user retention.

User Persona

After conducting my research, I created a user persona to better understand the target audience and their needs. This helped me visualize the regular Black Purple customer, their shopping behaviors, pain points, and expectations when browsing an e-commerce jewelry website.

Sketches

During the search I have found design patterns that made navigation, product discovery, and checking out feel seemless. This helped inspire my design decisions. I used my findings to start my sketches and wireframes before prototyping.

Style Guide

After finalizing my sketches, I wanted to finalize my design direction. I developed a style guide to ensure consistency in the user interface, branding, and overall aesthetic before moving into the prototyping phase.

Conclusion

The Black Purple website redesign addresses the key challenges identified during the UI audit, improving navigation, product discovery, and the checkout experience to create a more seamless shopping journey bootsting conversion rates, and keeping user retention. Through competitor analysis, user research, and a design system, the new interface enhances usability while maintaining the brand’s identity.

Future Steps

While the redesign has significantly improved the user experience, navigation, and checkout process, there are additional opportunities to further refine and enhance the Black Purple website. Moving forward, the next steps would be to conduct usability testing with real customers to gather insights on how they interact with the new design and Identify any remaining pain points and refine the interface based on user feedback.
